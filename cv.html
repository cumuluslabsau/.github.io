Things I believe in

Use appropriate technologies for the nature of problem and the phase of product maturity. Sometimes products require faster turnaround to explore an opportunity, other times it needs to be rock solid as a foundational piece upon which other services / features will be built. These require different approaches and oftentimes technologies to be effective.

Use scripting and automation to provide confidence. Many annoying time-consuming mistakes are done at the hands of people hastily trying to do the right thing. Automation in this context removes those manual steps thus allowing time to be better focussed on important items and can be broadly applied to include the creation of technical environments from local dev to production, data migration and testing functionality.

Intrinsic motivation is far more effective than extrinsic. Interesting and challenging propblems whose solutions yield results that have significant benefit from their use is an amazing motivator. The ability to work with driven people who support and challenge fosters innovative and effective teams, workplaces and companies. I've found that careful recruitment, focussed attention to colleagues and an embracing of continuous incremental improvement to be a formula for creating great outcomes.

There are many more, but I'll save the balance for a few blog posts in the not too distant future.
